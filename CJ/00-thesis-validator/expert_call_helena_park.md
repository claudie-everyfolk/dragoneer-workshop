# Expert Call — Helena Park, Former Deputy GC, Meridian Health Systems

**Date:** April 18, 2026
**Length:** 52 minutes
**Theme tag:** vertical-AI-for-legal
**Interviewer:** CJ (Partner, Private Investments)
**Background:** Helena spent 11 years in-house at Meridian (Fortune 200 health system), last 4 as Deputy GC managing a 38-person legal team. Left in Jan 2026. Now advising two early-stage legal-AI startups.

---

## Topic 1: How in-house legal teams are actually using AI in 2026

> "Look — two years ago this was novelty. People were running ChatGPT against contract drafts on their personal laptops because IT hadn't approved anything. Now? Every team I talk to has at least one sanctioned vendor for contract review and one for research. The behavior shift is real. The budget shift is starting."

> "The thing nobody's pricing in correctly: it's not 'AI replaces paralegals.' It's that a Deputy GC like me used to need a team of six to do contract triage across 4,000 vendor agreements. Now I can do it with two people and a tool like Lumen or Eveline.ai. That changes the org chart, but more importantly it changes who has buying power — it stops being procurement and starts being the GC."

## Topic 2: What's working vs. what's still broken

> "Contract review and redlining — solid. The good products genuinely save 60-70% of the time on a standard NDA or MSA review. I'd bet on that category."

> "Legal research — mixed. Westlaw and Lexis have caught up faster than people expected. The independent startups are getting squeezed in the middle market. I would not be betting on a pure-play legal research startup right now unless they have a real moat in a specific practice area."

> "Matter management and intake — completely unsolved. Every GC I know hates their matter management system. There's a real opening here and I don't see anyone winning it yet."

## Topic 3: Specific companies you've seen in the market

> "Eveline.ai — I love what they're doing on contract lifecycle. We piloted them at Meridian. The product is genuinely differentiated because they trained on healthcare-specific contract corpora. Vertical-within-vertical is the play."

> "Lumen Legal — fine product, but their go-to-market is broken. They're trying to sell to BigLaw, which is the worst possible buyer. BigLaw will pilot you forever and never buy. I'd worry about their burn."

> "ClauseIQ — I've heard their name but never seen them in a real deal. The marketing is loud, the deployments seem thin. I'd want to see actual signed enterprise contracts before I believed the hype."

> "Helix Counsel — they reached out to me about advising. Pre-product. Founders are real — both ex-Cravath, both technical. Too early to call but they're asking the right questions about workflow vs. point solution."

## Topic 4: What the expert is most bullish on

> "The wedge that wins is workflow ownership, not feature parity. If you're a startup and your strategy is 'we have better AI than incumbent X' — you're going to lose. If your strategy is 'we own the contract intake-to-execution workflow end-to-end and AI is the thing that makes it 10x faster,' you have a chance."

> "Verticalization matters more than people think. Healthcare legal, financial services legal, insurance legal — these are different products. A generalist legal AI tool is a worse product than a healthcare-specific legal AI tool, every time."

## Topic 5: What would change your mind

> "If Westlaw or Lexis acquires one of the top three contract review startups in the next 12 months, that compresses the independent opportunity dramatically."

> "If GCs start losing budget authority back to procurement — which could happen in a downturn — the buying dynamic flips and the startups suffer."

> "If the foundation model providers (Anthropic, OpenAI) ship legal-specific products directly, that's a real threat to the application layer."
