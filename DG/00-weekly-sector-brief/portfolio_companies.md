# Portfolio Companies — Vertical SaaS for Logistics

These are the four companies I currently cover and hold positions in. Use this as the canonical list for the per-company section of the brief — the other CSVs include both portfolio and non-portfolio names for context.

## Freightline

Series C portfolio company. We led the round in late 2024 at a $1.1B post. They sell a freight-management SaaS platform to mid-market shippers — TMS plus carrier marketplace plus settlement. Strong growth out of the gate, NRR consistently above 115 through 2025. Board observer seat. Last board meeting flagged customer concentration risk (top 5 accounts ~32% of ARR). CEO is a second-time founder, well regarded; sales org is the question mark.

## Cargomatic

Series B portfolio company. We participated in the 2025 round at $480M post. They run a logistics intelligence platform — pricing, capacity, and analytics for brokers and shippers. Recently leaned into AI as a strategic wedge. NRR is the best in our portfolio for this sector. Founder-led. Strong product culture. Our thesis was that the data moat compounds; the AI VP hire is consistent with that.

## ShipSense

Series B portfolio company. We participated in 2024 at $310M post. Embedded analytics for third-party logistics providers (3PLs). Partnership-driven GTM has been the bet — they win when a 3PL embeds them across their customer base. Quieter than the others but executing. New CRO suggests they're now ready to push into direct enterprise.

## Portlink

Series A portfolio company. We led in 2023 at $90M post. Port and terminal operations software — narrower TAM than the rest of the portfolio. Slower growth, but we underwrote a longer ramp. Recent customer satisfaction softness is a yellow flag. The flat extension round was structured to give them 18 more months of runway without resetting the cap table; we participated pro-rata.
