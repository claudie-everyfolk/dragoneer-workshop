# Weekly Sector Brief — Vertical SaaS for Logistics

I'm a Partner covering vertical SaaS for logistics. Every week I'm trying to keep current on the sector while also tracking what's changing competitively across the portfolio companies I cover. The inputs arrive scattered — press hits, hiring news, quarterly KPI updates, analyst chatter — and by the time I've stitched them together myself, the IC meeting is tomorrow. I want to stop doing that synthesis by hand.

I've pulled together four files that represent a typical week of inputs: news headlines across the sector, hiring signals (key hires and departures), a financial snapshot of the companies I track, and a short markdown describing the portfolio companies I own. I want you to read all four and produce one decision-ready brief.

The point is not a news summary. The point is connecting signals — when a portfolio company has a hiring departure AND a missed quarter AND a competitor just raised, that's a thread I need to pull. Surface those threads.

Using the attached sample data files — `sector_news.csv`, `hiring_signals.csv`, `financial_updates.csv`, and `portfolio_companies.md` — build me a one-page Weekly Sector Brief that includes:

1. **Top-line sector pulse** — 3 bullets on what moved this week across the sector overall (themes, not headlines).
2. **Per-portfolio-company section** — for each company in `portfolio_companies.md`: what changed this week (news + hiring + financial signals, cross-referenced), what it means competitively, and what action it implies for me.
3. **Ranked urgency** — sort the per-company sections by urgency (high / medium / low) with a one-line reason for each ranking.
4. **Cross-signal callouts** — any company where two or more signals point the same direction (e.g., negative news + key departure + soft KPIs). Flag these explicitly at the top.
5. **Watchlist** — non-portfolio companies in the data that are doing something I should track (a competitor raising, an adjacent player making a meaningful hire).
6. **Source trace** — at the bottom, a short list confirming which file each claim came from so I can spot-check.

## Why this matters

This connects three workflows I currently do in three places: scanning sector news, asking management for hiring updates, and reviewing quarterly KPIs. Stitching them into one ranked view means I walk into IC and portfolio check-ins already knowing where the action is — instead of reacting to whichever signal happened to surface most recently. The urgency ranking is the unlock: it tells me what to do today versus what can wait.

## Iteration hooks

- If the brief feels too long, ask for a "headline-only" version that fits on a phone screen.
- If the urgency ranking feels off, push back: "Why did you rank Company X high when their financials improved?" Make it justify.
- Ask it to re-rank the portfolio section by "biggest delta from last week's expectation" instead of urgency, and compare the two views.

## Stretch goals

- Add a competitive-positioning matrix — pick two axes from the data (e.g., growth rate vs. hiring velocity) and place each portfolio company on it.
- Generate a 3-bullet "questions to ask the CEO this week" list per portfolio company, grounded in the signals above.
- Turn the full brief into a Monday-morning email draft addressed to the deal team, with the cross-signal callouts in the subject line.
- Have it write a small script that, given new versions of these four files next week, regenerates the brief automatically.
