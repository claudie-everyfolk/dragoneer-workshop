# DG — Weekly Sector Brief

## Profile

- **Role:** Partner, Investment
- **Seniority:** C-suite
- **Daily work:** Diligence, sector coverage, ongoing portfolio engagement
- **Documented pain:** Keeping current on sector developments; tracking competitive landscape changes across many portfolio companies
- **Inferred pain:** Information arrives in fragments — press hits, hiring rumors, financial updates, analyst chatter — and never lands in one place at one time. By the time DG has stitched it together himself, the IC meeting is tomorrow and the read is reactive instead of strategic.
- **AI maturity:** Medium — curious, follows the space, but not yet hands-on with Claude Code. First-time builder.

## Session Build

- **Exercise name:** Weekly Sector Brief
- **Slug:** `00-weekly-sector-brief`
- **Complexity:** Medium
- **Estimated time:** 30–40 minutes for the base build
- **Deliverable:** A one-page, decision-ready weekly brief that fuses news, hiring signals, and financial updates into a per-company "what changed / what it means / what to do" view, sorted by urgency.

### Stretches

1. Add a competitive-positioning matrix (where each portfolio company sits vs. peers on two axes the model picks from the data).
2. Generate a 3-bullet "questions to ask the CEO this week" list per portfolio company.
3. Turn the brief into a Monday-morning email draft addressed to the deal team.

## Facilitator Notes

- **Support level:** Light. Check in at minute 5 and minute 15. He's curious and capable, but this may be his first paste-and-run with Claude Code.
- **Pairing:** If he stalls in the first 5 minutes on mechanics (where to paste, how to attach files), pair him briefly with a facilitator. Once the first output renders, he'll move fast.
- **Approach:** Lead with the "wow" — the moment four scattered files collapse into one ranked brief is the unlock. Don't over-explain upfront; let him see it.
- **Data confidence:** Fully synthetic. Sector is "vertical SaaS for logistics" — generic enough to feel real without resembling any actual portfolio.
- **Risks:**
  - He may try to read every file manually before running the prompt. Encourage him to paste first, read the output, then dig into the source files only if he wants to spot-check.
  - He may ask "is this hallucinating?" — good instinct. The validatable answer: every claim in the brief should trace back to a row in the CSVs. Spot-check 2–3.
- **Finish-early move:** Push him to Stretch 3 (Monday email draft). It maps directly to a workflow he already does manually.
- **What "good" looks like:** The brief surfaces at least one cross-signal he wouldn't have spotted from any single file — e.g. "Company X had a VP of Sales departure AND missed revenue guidance AND a competitor just raised — investigate." That's the synthesis moment.
