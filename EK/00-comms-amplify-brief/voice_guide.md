# Firm Voice Guide

Last updated by Comms. Internal use only. If a draft doesn't follow this guide, it doesn't go out.

## Core Voice Principles (firm-wide)

We are a long-duration investor. Our voice reflects that:

- **Patience over hype.** We do not say "transformational," "revolutionary," "game-changing," or any variant. We describe what is happening and let the reader draw conclusions.
- **Specifics over adjectives.** "Signed a multi-year contract with a top-three parcel carrier" beats "landed a huge customer."
- **We do not predict.** We say "we believe" or "we think" — not "will." Markets punish certainty and we don't trade it.
- **No founder worship in public.** We talk about companies, customers, and traction. Founders get praise privately or in their own words via quotes, not ours.
- **No competitor naming.** We do not name competitors of our portfolio companies in posts. If a piece of coverage names competitors, we don't quote that part.

## Things We Never Say

- "We're thrilled to announce…" (we're not press release wire)
- "Disrupting [industry]"
- "Backing the founders building the future"
- Anything with "10x," "step-change," or "next-gen"
- "Congrats to the team!" as a standalone — say *what* they did
- Anything that implies returns or fund performance

## Things We Do Say

- "Worth reading."
- "What this changes:" (followed by a specific thing)
- "We've been working with [company] for [N years] and what stands out is [specific observation]"
- Numbers, dates, customer types, contract terms when public

## Partner-by-Partner Voice Notes

### Partner A (Industrial / Logistics)
- **Tone:** Plain-spoken, almost flat. No exclamation marks ever. Sentences are short.
- **Default opener:** Often leads with a number or a customer. "Northwind shipped its 10,000th unit this quarter."
- **Avoid:** Anything that sounds like a victory lap. He hates "thrilled," "proud," "excited."
- **Length:** Short. Three or four sentences max on social.
- **Signature move:** Ends with one understated line — "More to come." or "Worth watching."

### Partner B (Life Sciences)
- **Tone:** Data-driven, deliberately precise. Will name the molecule, the indication, the trial phase.
- **Default opener:** A specific clinical or regulatory milestone, stated clinically.
- **Avoid:** Patient anecdotes (regulatory sensitivity). Anything that sounds like marketing for the therapy itself.
- **Length:** Medium. Five to seven sentences. He'll use a paragraph break.
- **Signature move:** Mentions the platform or mechanism, not just the asset. "What's notable is the platform's ability to…"

### Partner C (Climate / Infrastructure)
- **Tone:** Builder's voice. Talks about systems, grids, customers, deployment. Practical, not visionary.
- **Default opener:** A customer or a deployment milestone. "Pacific Northwest co-op went live on Meridian this week."
- **Avoid:** Climate-doom framing. Policy commentary. Anything that reads as political.
- **Length:** Medium-short. Four to six sentences.
- **Signature move:** A line about what the deployment unlocks operationally. "This is the kind of integration that takes utilities from quarterly to monthly planning."

### Partner D (Software / Consumer)
- **Tone:** Curious, observational, occasionally wry. Most likely of the partners to use a question as a hook.
- **Default opener:** A question or an observation about the category. "What does a coffee chain expansion look like in a tight consumer market?"
- **Avoid:** Insider jargon. She writes to a smart outsider.
- **Length:** Medium. Five to eight sentences.
- **Signature move:** Pulls one specific operational detail into the broader narrative. "12 new locations is small. The fact that they're all within 200 miles of existing locations is the actual story."
