# Press Coverage Tracker — Q1 2026

Selected press hits for the universe under review. Tone column reflects the dominant framing of each piece, not its accuracy.

---

## NRTH

**The Financial Ledger, March 18, 2026 — "NRTH's Whitfield: The Quiet Compounder"**
Profile of CEO Dana Whitfield. Notes that NRTH has not held a single sell-side analyst day in 4 years and that Whitfield "considers investor relations a distraction from the work." Reporter notes the company's 5-year free cash flow per share CAGR of 22%.
*Tone: positive, substantive*

**Markets Desk, April 4, 2026 — "Why NRTH Walked Away From a 200M Deal"**
Mid-market competitor began deep discounting. NRTH's CFO confirmed on background they would not match. Two former employees quoted, both favorably, on Whitfield's discipline.
*Tone: positive, neutral framing*

## VLTC

**The Industry Wire, January 22, 2026 — "VLTC Faces Quiet SEC Inquiry Over Revenue Practices"**
Reports the informal inquiry disclosed in the 8-K. Three former VLTC sales operations employees, on background, describe pressure to pull deals forward at quarter end during the period under inquiry.
*Tone: negative, investigative*

**Buy-Side Brief, March 11, 2026 — "VLTC's Hsu Insists Growth Story Intact"**
Coverage of conference appearance. CEO repeated that "the underlying signals are strong" four times across a 30-minute fireside chat. Two sell-side analysts quoted noting concerns about CFO Lansing's extended tenure announcement.
*Tone: skeptical*

## KPRA

**Sector Weekly, March 16, 2026 — "KPRA's Acquisition Math: A Test of Discipline"**
Analysis of the 240M acquisition. Argues 4.1x trailing revenue is reasonable for the asset class but flags that 60/40 stock/cash mix is unusual for a company whose stock has been a low-volatility compounder. Concludes "this will be a useful data point on Ferreira's capital allocation instincts."
*Tone: neutral, substantive*

**The Financial Ledger, April 9, 2026 — "KPRA's Cohort Disclosure Is the Real Story This Quarter"**
Notes that KPRA's voluntary disclosure of cohort retention by customer size is unusual for the industry and gives shareholders a tool to triangulate management commentary on churn.
*Tone: positive*

## BRDG

**Markets Desk, February 28, 2026 — "BRDG's Vossen Plans Four Years of Margin Pain"**
Coverage of 10-K disclosure. Quotes Vossen: "If shareholders need linear margin expansion every quarter to stay invested, this is not the right vehicle for them." Stock down 4% on the day, recovered within a week.
*Tone: respectful, slightly skeptical*

**Buy-Side Brief, April 14, 2026 — "BRDG's Buyback Authorization Reads Like a Value Investor's Memo"**
Notes the 8-K language tying repurchase activity to a stated intrinsic value range. Calls it "the most disciplined buyback authorization disclosed by a public software company this year."
*Tone: very positive*

---

## SOLN

**The IPO Beat, March 6, 2026 — "SOLN's CRO Exit Raises Familiar Questions"**
Coverage of the Chief Revenue Officer departure. Notes the 14-month tenure is short and the "personal reasons" language tracks with three other post-IPO go-to-market leadership departures the publication has covered in the past 18 months. Notes SOLN has not provided updated CAC payback target since IPO.
*Tone: negative, pattern-matching*

**Markets Desk, April 30, 2026 — "Reading SOLN's CAC Footnote"**
Examines the methodology change disclosed in the 10-Q footnote. Argues that under the prior IPO-era methodology, CAC payback would have been 31 months versus the reported 26. "The number that matters is the one investors were promised at IPO."
*Tone: critical*

---

## MERI

**Turnaround Quarterly, March 22, 2026 — "MERI's Cohen Is Three for Three"**
Profile of CEO Iris Cohen at the midpoint of the stated turnaround plan. Notes she has hit each of the three operational milestones laid out 18 months ago, in order, on time. Notes the voluntary 30% salary reduction in 2024 has still not been restored.
*Tone: very positive*

**The Financial Ledger, April 11, 2026 — "MERI's Buyback Timing Looks Like a Master Class"**
Examines the Q1 buyback executed at an average price 18% below current levels. Notes Cohen publicly stated on the prior quarter's call that she viewed the stock as "materially undervalued" two weeks before the repurchase.
*Tone: very positive*

## TGRA

**The Industry Wire, March 26, 2026 — "TGRA's CFO Exit Catches the Market Off Guard"**
Coverage of the abrupt CFO departure. Notes TGRA had reaffirmed full-year guidance only six weeks earlier. Three sell-side analysts pulled their price targets pending more information.
*Tone: negative, urgent*

**Buy-Side Brief, April 3, 2026 — "TGRA's Special RSU Grant Filed Three Weeks Before Guidance Withdrawal"**
Investigation into the 8.5M RSU retention grant to the CEO disclosed in the proxy. Filing predates the guidance withdrawal by three weeks. Two governance experts quoted raising questions about sequencing and board oversight.
*Tone: very negative*

**Markets Desk, April 30, 2026 — "TGRA's Recurring Revenue Math Doesn't Quite Work"**
Examines the redefinition of recurring revenue in the latest 10-Q to include certain one-time professional services. Estimates that under the prior definition, recurring revenue declined 6% YoY versus the reported 1% growth.
*Tone: very negative, investigative*
